# Social Content Skill

## Purpose
Create, optimize, and distribute compelling social media content across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, and other platforms. This skill enables AI agents to craft platform-specific posts, design carousel content, write engaging threads, produce video scripts, build personal brands, repurpose content across channels, manage hashtag strategy, and measure social performance to drive engagement, reach, and conversions.

## When to Use
- Build thought leadership and brand authority in niche or industry
- Generate leads through organic social engagement
- Drive traffic to blog posts, products, or landing pages
- Create recurring content for consistent audience building
- Repurpose existing content (blog posts, podcasts, videos) for social distribution
- Test messaging and positioning before investing in paid ads
- Build community and engagement around brand or personal brand
- Need 20+ pieces of content per month without large in-house team
- Want to increase social reach and follower count
- Planning content calendar for seasonal campaigns or launches

## Core Frameworks & Knowledge

### Platform-Specific Content Best Practices

#### LinkedIn
- **Best For**: B2B, professional services, career advice, industry news, thought leadership
- **Audience**: 930M users; professionals, executives, job seekers (highly engaged)
- **Post Types**: Long-form articles, industry insights, career advice, company updates
- **Optimal Length**: 150-200 characters for social proof; 1,000+ words for long-form articles (native articles rank well)
- **Posting Frequency**: 2-4 times per week (daily risks algorithm suppression)
- **Engagement Drivers**: Controversial takes, personal stories, data/stats, industry predictions
- **Format Success**: Native articles (9x higher engagement), carousels (2nd), text posts (3rd)
- **Hashtag Strategy**: 3-5 niche hashtags (e.g., #MarketingStrategy #B2BLeadGen); LinkedIn algorithm favors niche over broad

#### Twitter/X
- **Best For**: News, commentary, real-time engagement, community building, developer marketing
- **Audience**: 550M users; tech-savvy, news-focused, debate-oriented
- **Post Types**: Threads (stories, tips, threads), takes, retweets with commentary, replies to trending topics
- **Optimal Length**: 280 characters (but threads can be longer)
- **Posting Frequency**: 3-10 times per day (Twitter rewards active accounts; audience checks multiple times daily)
- **Engagement Drivers**: Controversial opinions, valuable tips (threads), real-time commentary on news, relatable humor
- **Format Success**: Threads (high engagement, discoverability), single tweets with media (images drive 35% more engagement), polls
- **Hashtag Strategy**: 1-2 broad hashtags max (discovery); Twitter audience scrolls through feeds, not searching

#### Instagram
- **Best For**: Visual storytelling, lifestyle, product showcase, personal branding, B2C (some B2B)
- **Audience**: 2B users; visual-first, lifestyle-focused, younger demographic (but aging up)
- **Post Types**: Carousel (5-10 images), Reels (short video, 15-90 seconds), Stories (ephemeral, 24 hours)
- **Optimal Length**: Captions: 150-200 characters (sweet spot before "more"); Reels: 15-30 seconds
- **Posting Frequency**: 3-5 times per week (Reels 2-3x weekly for reach); Stories daily
- **Engagement Drivers**: Behind-the-scenes, before/after, user-generated content, Reels (Reels get 67% more reach than static posts)
- **Format Success**: Reels (highest reach), Carousel, Stories (high engagement from followers)
- **Hashtag Strategy**: 20-30 relevant hashtags (spread between captions and 1st comment); mix popular (100K+) and niche (1K-10K)

#### TikTok
- **Best For**: Entertainment, trending audio, younger audience, viral potential, product education (creative)
- **Audience**: 1.6B users; 60% under 24, but growing older; highly engaged, algorithm-driven
- **Post Types**: Short-form video (15-60 seconds), trending sounds/music, challenges, education disguised as entertainment
- **Optimal Length**: 21-34 seconds (sweet spot for retention); longer videos need hook in first 3 seconds
- **Posting Frequency**: 1-3 times per day (algorithm favors frequency; freshness important)
- **Engagement Drivers**: Trending sounds, trending effects, original content, humor, relatability, "watch til end"
- **Format Success**: Trending audio + original hook (highest), before/after or transformation, educational content (creative angle)
- **Hashtag Strategy**: 3-5 hashtags (some trending, some niche); TikTok algorithm doesn't favor hashtags as much as content quality

### Hook Writing & Attention Grabbing

The first 1-3 seconds determines if content is watched or scrolled past.

#### Hook Formulas
1. **Curiosity Gap**
   - "I spent 30 days doing this... here's what happened"
   - "Most people don't know this about [topic]"
   - "Everyone's doing [wrong thing], but actually..."

2. **Bold Statement / Hot Take**
   - "[Conventional wisdom] is wrong"
   - "The best [category] isn't [expected answer], it's..."
   - "Stop [common mistake]; do this instead"

3. **Promise / Benefit**
   - "Save 3 hours per week with this [tactic]"
   - "How to [desired outcome] in [timeframe]"
   - "The #1 reason [problem] isn't [expected cause]"

4. **Pattern Interrupt / Unexpected**
   - "Wait... [unexpected fact]"
   - "I was wrong about [topic]"
   - Contrast image or unexpected statement

5. **Question / Direct Address**
   - "What if [provocative scenario]?"
   - "Ever felt [relatable emotion]?"
   - "Notice how [observation]?"

6. **Story Hook**
   - "Last week, I [interesting event]..."
   - "One of our customers [unexpected success]..."
   - "I used to [relatable struggle]..."

#### Hook Best Practices
- **Lead with hook**: First sentence must stop scrolling (not context or preamble)
- **Platform-specific delivery**:
  - LinkedIn: Hook in first line of text or headline
  - Twitter: Hook in first tweet of thread or first 30 chars
  - Instagram: Hook in caption or visual
  - TikTok: Hook must be visual + audio (trending sound or on-screen text)
- **Avoid clickbait**: Promise must deliver; misleading hooks destroy credibility
- **A/B test hooks**: Track which hooks drive highest engagement rate for your content type

### Carousel Design Best Practices

Carousels (multi-image posts) outperform single images on Facebook/Instagram and are effective on LinkedIn.

#### Design Principles
1. **Clear Title Slide**
   - Slide 1: Headline, benefit, or hook text (people must know what to expect)
   - Format: Bold text, high contrast, readable at small size
   - Example: "5 SEO Mistakes Killing Your Traffic" or "How We 3x'd SaaS Growth"

2. **One Concept Per Slide**
   - Don't cram 3-4 ideas on one slide; breaks scanability
   - Ideal slide progression: Intro â†’ Insight 1 â†’ Insight 2 â†’ Insight 3 â†’ Conclusion/CTA

3. **Consistent Visual Style**
   - Same background color/texture across all slides
   - Same fonts (max 2 font families)
   - Consistent color palette (3-4 colors)
   - Branded elements (logo, fonts, colors) reinforces brand

4. **Readable Text**
   - Max 3-4 lines of text per slide
   - Font size: Min 24pt (readable at 1/4 screen size)
   - High contrast (dark text on light bg or vice versa)
   - No text in bottom corners (Instagram UI covers it)

5. **Visual Hierarchy**
   - 1 headline per slide (largest text)
   - 1-2 sub-points (smaller text)
   - Supporting visual (icon, chart, image)

6. **Call-to-Action Slide**
   - Final slide: Clear CTA and next step
   - "DM for [offer]" or "Link in bio for [resource]" or "Follow for more"
   - Make it distinct from other slides (color, format)

### Thread Writing (Twitter/LinkedIn)

Threads are powerful for storytelling, education, and building audience.

#### Thread Structure
1. **Hook Tweet** (Tweet 1)
   - Bold statement, question, or curiosity gap
   - "I spent 6 months analyzing 500+ SaaS landing pages. Here's the pattern that separates $10M from $100M companies."
   - Should make people reply or retweet immediately

2. **Intro Context** (Tweet 2)
   - Briefly explain why they should care
   - "Most landing pages are missing this one thing..."
   - Build stakes (time, money, opportunity)

3. **Main Content** (Tweets 3-N)
   - Each tweet one insight, one story beat, one data point
   - Progressive disclosure: Each tweet builds on previous
   - Pattern: Setup â†’ Insight â†’ Evidence
   - Threads of 5-7 tweets perform better than 10+ (attention fatigue)

4. **Conclusion** (Final tweet)
   - Summarize takeaway or moral
   - CTA (follow for more, share, reply with take)
   - Create pattern: "Tweet 1 and thread X below ðŸ‘‡"

#### Thread Engagement Tactics
- **Pin hook tweet**: Keep Thread 1 at top of replies; people scroll first reply to see full thread
- **Quote your own thread**: Retweet hook tweet with commentary 2-3 days later (reach fresh audience)
- **Reply to own tweets**: Adds weight to thread in algorithm
- **Use polls**: Embed poll in thread to boost engagement; people must click through
- **Add data/images**: Text-only threads underperform; add image or chart every 2-3 tweets

### Hashtag Strategy

Hashtags increase discoverability but impact varies by platform.

#### Platform-Specific Hashtag Strategy
1. **LinkedIn**
   - Niche over broad: #MarketingStrategy vs. #Marketing
   - 3-5 hashtags optimal (more doesn't help; may suppress reach)
   - Algorithm values relevance; irrelevant hashtags hurt
   - Include 1 personal/brand hashtag for community

2. **Twitter**
   - 1-2 hashtags max (discovery is minimal; Twitter audience uses timeline/search bar)
   - Trending hashtags for real-time engagement (e.g., #SaaS)
   - Avoid over-tagging; looks spammy

3. **Instagram**
   - 20-30 hashtags (split: put in caption or 1st comment post-engagement to avoid spam appearance)
   - Mix strategy: 5 popular (100K+), 10 medium (10K-100K), 10 niche (1K-10K)
   - Create branded hashtag (#YourBrandInsights) for community/UGC

4. **TikTok**
   - 3-5 hashtags (include 1 trending for algorithmic boost)
   - Algorithm relies less on hashtags; focus on watch-through rate
   - Use trending sounds more than trending hashtags

#### Hashtag Research Tools & Tactics
- **Search volume**: Use platform search bar to see volume and type of content using hashtag
- **Relevance**: Only use hashtags where your content fits naturally
- **Create branded hashtag**: Build community using #YourBrand or #YourCampaign
- **Avoid**: Generic (#instagood), spam-flagged (#follow4follow), or dead hashtags

### Content Repurposing for Social

One piece of content (blog post, video, podcast) can generate 10+ social posts.

#### Repurposing Framework
1. **Blog Post â†’ Social Pieces**
   - Main insight + CTA (as carousel intro or thread hook)
   - 3-5 supporting statistics (quoted graphic post)
   - Contrarian take or hot take (hook for thread)
   - FAQ from blog (Q&A Instagram Stories or TikTok)
   - Pull quotes from blog post (quote graphics)

2. **Video/Podcast â†’ Social Pieces**
   - Clip best quote (15-30 sec TikTok/Reel)
   - Key statistic or insight (graphic post)
   - Guest highlight or expert take (tagged/thread)
   - Full episode link with testimonial (Story or post)

3. **Infographic â†’ Social Pieces**
   - Full graphic (single post)
   - Individual sections (carousel slides)
   - Key insight + stat (quote graphic)
   - "Swipe" or "Save this" CTA post

#### Repurposing Timeline
- **Day 0**: Publish original content (blog, video, etc.)
- **Day 1**: Share to primary platform (LinkedIn article, YouTube video)
- **Day 2-3**: Create 3-5 derivative social posts (quotes, stats, hooks)
- **Day 5-7**: Share derivative posts across secondary platforms
- **Week 2-4**: Repost top performer with updated caption

### Building a Personal Brand on Social

Personal brands drive more engagement and trust than corporate accounts.

#### Personal Branding Pillars
1. **Expertise**: Share knowledge, insights, frameworks from your domain
2. **Personality**: Be yourself; authentic personalities attract followers
3. **Perspective**: Unique viewpoint (contrarian takes, unique synthesis of ideas)
4. **Generosity**: Share freely without gating (builds trust, authority)
5. **Community**: Engage, reply, celebrate others; don't broadcast only

#### Content Mix for Personal Brand
- **60% Value**: Educational, actionable insights, frameworks, data
- **20% Personal**: Behind-the-scenes, wins, failures, honest moments
- **15% Engagement**: Asking questions, replying to comments, community
- **5% Promotional**: Subtle CTA (link in bio, book recommendations, your product/service)

#### Personal Brand Best Practices
- **Consistent perspective**: Pick 2-3 topics you're known for; become the go-to voice
- **Authentic voice**: Use your language, humor, values (not corporate copy)
- **Show your work**: Share processes, case studies, learnings; transparency builds trust
- **Engage genuinely**: Reply thoughtfully to followers; build relationships
- **Consistency**: Post regularly (3-5 times per week) and commit for 3-6 months before assessing

### Measuring Social Performance

#### Key Metrics by Goal
1. **Awareness/Reach**
   - Impressions (how many people saw content)
   - Reach (unique people)
   - Share of voice (your content vs. competitors in topic)

2. **Engagement**
   - Engagement rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) Ã· Impressions Ã— 100
   - Target: 1-5% for established accounts; 5%+ indicates strong content
   - Individual metrics: Likes (low-friction), Comments (thoughtful), Shares (highly valuable)

3. **Growth**
   - Followers gained per month (trend over 3+ months)
   - Follower growth rate = (New followers Ã· Total followers) Ã— 100
   - Benchmark: 1-2% monthly growth for consistent posting

4. **Conversions**
   - Clicks to landing page (from bio link, CTAs)
   - Leads generated (email signups, contact form submissions)
   - Sales (if directly attributable)
   - Cost per lead/sale (ad spend on social vs. conversions)

5. **Audience Health**
   - Follower quality (are they engaged? Are they your target audience?)
   - Comment sentiment (positive, neutral, negative)
   - Share of followers' attention (are they following competitors too?)

#### Analytics Tools & Dashboards
- **Native platforms**: LinkedIn Analytics, Twitter Analytics, Instagram Insights, TikTok Analytics
- **Third-party**: Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprout Social (cross-platform tracking)
- **Custom dashboard**: Spreadsheet or Looker dashboard tracking key metrics over time

#### Performance Benchmarking & Optimization
- **Baseline**: Track metrics for 30 days to establish baseline (engagement rate, CTR, follower growth)
- **Top performers**: Identify top 20% of content (by engagement, reach, or conversions)
- **Analyze winners**: What's different? Topic? Hook? Time posted? Format?
- **Double down**: Create more content similar to top performers
- **Remove underperformers**: Pause content receiving <0.5% engagement rate

## Process (Step-by-Step)

### 1. Social Strategy & Audience Definition
- [ ] Define primary goal: Lead generation, brand awareness, community, sales, or thought leadership
- [ ] Identify 2-3 primary platforms based on audience presence (LinkedIn for B2B, Instagram for B2C/lifestyle, Twitter for developers/tech)
- [ ] Research target audience on chosen platforms:
  - Demographics (age, job title, industry)
  - Pain points and interests
  - How they consume content (short-form video, long-form articles, real-time chat)
  - Competitors and accounts they follow
- [ ] Define content pillars (2-3 main topics you'll consistently share on)
  - Example for SaaS marketer: Product growth, content marketing, personal insights
  - Ensure pillars align with audience interests and your expertise
- [ ] Set baseline metrics (current followers, engagement rate, reach)
- [ ] Define success metrics (engagement rate target, follower growth rate, conversion rate)

### 2. Content Calendar & Planning
- [ ] Create content calendar for 4-12 weeks (Google Sheets or Asana)
  - Map content by platform, date, pillar, format
  - Plan content themes weekly or by campaign
- [ ] Identify 3-5 cornerstone pieces to develop:
  - 1-2 big article or video projects (repurposed to 10+ social posts)
  - 1-2 personal stories or case studies
  - Regular tips, insights, or educational content
- [ ] Set posting frequency:
  - LinkedIn: 2-4 posts/week
  - Twitter: 3-10 posts/day
  - Instagram: 3-5 posts/week + Daily Stories
  - TikTok: 1-3 posts/day
- [ ] Pre-create content batch (2-4 weeks of posts at once)
  - Reduces friction, improves consistency
  - Batch filming/writing saves time

### 3. Hook & Copy Writing
- [ ] For each post, write 3 hook variations (choose strongest)
  - Use hook formulas: Curiosity gap, bold statement, promise, pattern interrupt, question, story
- [ ] Write body copy aligned to platform:
  - LinkedIn: Professional, longer-form, industry insights
  - Twitter: Conversational, direct, sometimes casual
  - Instagram: Storytelling, aspirational, caption-focused
- [ ] Add CTAs:
  - Soft (reply, share, save): drives engagement
  - Medium (follow, link in bio): builds audience
  - Hard (DM, sign up, buy): conversion-focused
- [ ] Proofread and ensure brand voice consistent

### 4. Visual Design & Creative Development
- [ ] For static posts: Design or source high-quality image
  - Ensure readable, on-brand, relevant to copy
  - Optimize for platform (LinkedIn: 1200x627px, Instagram: 1080x1080px)
  - Use consistent color palette and fonts
- [ ] For carousels: Design 5-10 slides
  - Title slide (hook)
  - 3-5 content slides (one insight per slide)
  - CTA slide
  - Use Canva, Figma, or design tool with brand template
- [ ] For video: Film or source video content
  - Hook in first 3 seconds
  - Add captions/text overlay (readable without sound)
  - Optimize length per platform (21-34s for Reels, 15-60s for TikTok)
  - Use trending audio (Instagram, TikTok)
- [ ] Batch create graphics: Weekly batch of 5-10 designs saves time

### 5. Posting & Scheduling
- [ ] Use scheduling tool (Buffer, Hootsuite, or native platform scheduler)
  - Schedule posts 1-2 weeks in advance to automate publishing
  - Maintain consistency without daily manual posting
- [ ] Optimal posting times (test and adjust based on platform analytics):
  - LinkedIn: Weekday mornings (8-10am)
  - Twitter: Weekday 9am-5pm (high traffic, conversation-focused)
  - Instagram: Evenings (6-9pm) weekday or morning (8-11am) weekends
  - TikTok: Post in morning, evening, night (audience active throughout day)
- [ ] Publish for your audience's timezone (not your own)
- [ ] For live/trending content: Skip scheduler; post immediately while trending

### 6. Engagement & Community Management
- [ ] Daily: Check notifications, replies, DMs
  - Reply to every comment within 24 hours (boosts algorithm)
  - Thoughtful replies (not just "thanks!") encourage more engagement
  - Ask follow-up questions to extend conversations
- [ ] Engage with others' content:
  - Follow and engage with 10-20 relevant accounts 3x per week
  - Comment genuinely on posts in your industry (networking + reach)
  - Retweet/share others' content with added value (builds goodwill)
- [ ] Build community:
  - Ask questions to encourage replies
  - Start weekly thread or series (recurring content improves audience)
  - Celebrate follower wins or tag collaborators
- [ ] Monitor comments for sentiment (negative feedback deserves thoughtful response)

### 7. Analytics Review & Optimization (Weekly/Monthly)
- [ ] Weekly check:
  - Look at top 3 posts (by engagement, reach, or conversions)
  - Identify patterns: Topic? Hook? Format? Time?
  - Check engagement rate trend (stable, growing, or declining?)
- [ ] Monthly deep dive:
  - Calculate engagement rate, reach per post, follower growth rate
  - Identify top 20% of content; analyze what made it successful
  - Identify bottom 20%; assess if topic is wrong, hook is weak, or format isn't working
  - Plan content changes based on learnings
- [ ] Quarterly strategy assessment:
  - Are we hitting our goals (followers, engagement, conversions)?
  - Should we adjust platforms, content pillars, or posting frequency?
  - Test new formats (video, threads, series) and measure impact

### 8. Content Repurposing & Scaling
- [ ] After publishing cornerstone content (blog, video), create derivative social posts:
  - Extract 5-7 key quotes or insights
  - Pull statistics or surprising findings
  - Create carousel from sections
  - Film short "teaser" video
- [ ] Schedule derivative posts across 2-4 weeks (don't dump all at once)
- [ ] Reuse best-performing posts:
  - Repurpose top post with new angle/caption every 30-90 days
  - Test hook variations of high-performing posts
- [ ] Document repurposing template for your top content types
  - Blog post â†’ 8 social posts (template)
  - Video â†’ 5 social posts (template)
  - Saves time on future repurposing
